Ebony Alert · SB 673 · October 2023

California built a state alert system just for missing Black persons.

  • Senate Bill 673 was introduced by Sen. Steven Bradford (D-Gardena) and signed by Governor Newsom on October 8, 2023. Effective January 1, 2024. Now codified as California Government Code Section 8594.14.
  • The law authorizes California Highway Patrol to activate an Ebony Alert for missing Black persons ages 12–25. Electronic highway signs. Dissemination to TV, radio, and social media.
  • California’s other emergency alerts: Amber (any child, race-neutral), Silver (age 65+), Feather (indigenous), and now Ebony.
  • There is no White-specific alert. The implicit premise: Amber already favors white children, so a Black-only counterweight is required.
  • The bill passed with zero votes against.

Proposition 57 · 2016

The law that calls domestic violence nonviolent.

Proposition 57 passed in California in 2016 under the framing of prison overcrowding and racial justice. It authorized earlier parole for offenders convicted of "nonviolent" felonies. Under California's statutory definitions retained by Prop 57, "nonviolent" includes domestic violence, drive-by shootings, assault with a deadly weapon, and rape of an unconscious victim. The list is not a typo. The measure did not redefine these offenses — it accepted the existing narrow statutory category of "violent" (which covers only a handful of specific offenses) and expanded early release to everything outside that list.

For prisoners serving time for an offense in the newly expanded "nonviolent" bucket, good-time credit eligibility rose from 20% to 50%. Sentences effectively halved. DAs were not required to be notified of the resulting early releases.
